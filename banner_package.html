Research & Development

Believe Supplements

I choose to do Believe Supplements as they use top quality and clean ingredients. They also happen to be one of my favorite supplement companies.

Tree Canada

I choose to do Tree Canada because as an environmental advocate, planting more trees will help carbon reduce atmospheric CO2. In addition, I feel as a collective action we could make a difference in the world.

Believe Supplements - Miami Vice Pre-workout

The container's heavy Miami nightlight vibe inspired me to make banner ads that matched its aesthetic. Considering the consumer demographic, I knew vibrant colors and a fun atmosphere would be essential to creating an eye-catching ad.

Implementations
  • To evoke a disco atmosphere, I illuminated the ad to make it look like a sign with neon lights.
  • Using the same pink and blue colours to match the colour scheme of the container to associate energy, youth, and vitality, which align with the product's effect.
  • I used a Miami Vice-inspired font to complemented the ad.

Tree Canada - Non-Profit Agency.

Given Tree Canada is a charity I choose to go with a more minimal approach. With climate change being a real concern. I wanted to shred hope by using a tree sampling and a dove to symbolizing hope and a desire to contribute to a better planet.

Implementations
  • The dove, a symbol of peace and hope, references the biblical story of Noah's Ark, where its return signaled the end of the flood and the beginning of a new era.
  • The tree sapling represents growth and symbolizes the organization's commitment to a healthier planet, aligning with the slogan ‚ÄúGrowing for a better Planet."
  • The strong use of forest green colors reflects the organization's focus on environmental conservation and forestry.
Conclusion

A short project but a fun and engaging one nonetheless. Being creative is always enjoyable, especially when it involves companies that I'm a fan of. However, some challenges arose when working with small pixel dimensions, such as scaling company logos to fit and maintain aspect ratios, sizing typography, and creating an engaging CTA for potential customers. To overcome these obstacles, I used Adobe's Image Trace tool to convert the picture logos into SVGs, researched optimal typography sizes for banner ads, and got inspiration from Adobe Stock banner ad examples.
