Better Planet App

UI/UX Design

Project Overview

The Better Planet app is a recycling assistant app that allows users to simply snap a photo of any number of items. The app's AI will then determine if the items are recyclable and suggest nearby drop-off locations. Additionally, a points system will be in place where users can earn points for proper recycling. These points can be redeemed for rewards.

User Experience Blueprint

This phase involved gathering information to better understand user needs for the Better Planet App and identifying necessary functionalities through user centered design artifacts.

Exploration

As an environmental advocate, I've noticed that our society generates a significant amount of waste, often leading to overflowing landfills, polluted oceans, and loss of habit for both animals and humans. While recycling programs exist, there's lack of incentives to properly use them. That's why I created the Better Planet App. It uses AI to scan images and determine the recyclability of common household items. Additionally, it rewards users with points for recycling, which can be redeemed for e-gift cards.

Competitive Analysis

Although no direct competitors were found, the Better Planet App draws inspiration from ReceiptJar's reward system. By offering points for recycling, I aim to incentivize proper waste disposal. The app will collect data on recycling habits to improve its functionality and provide valuable insights on making recycling easier.

User Centered Design Artifacts

User Persona

After doing research on recycling programs in BC and finding common issues that others had in the downtown area. I decided to base my user persona on a 26-year-old investment banker from downtown Vancouver.

This persona represents a busy, environmentally conscious individual who is likely to engage with the app due to his concern about the environment and confusion about his recycling program in downtown Vancouver.

User Scenario

John Lee uses the Better Planet app's AI scan feature, "Recycle Bot," to scan and sorted piles of his items. The bot identifies recyclable items and provides information on proper disposal. The app also gives an estimate on the rewards for proper recycling.

User Goal: To clean his apartment by sorting items into donation, recyclable, and non-recyclable piles while minimizing the non-recyclable pile.

User Testing

Testing of the prototypes was conducted on the Maze platform. The user tests was done by 4 participants where feedback was gathered. Overall feedback was positive; however, the Maze report indicated a high number of user misclicks, confusing navigation, extended time to complete simple tasks, and a visually unappealing design. Here I discovered a significant revision of the app was needed.

To address these issues, I redesigned the app with a focus on simplicity and accessibility. I adjusted colors to evoke emotions and refined visual elements for better readability, following Jakob's Law. These changes aim to create a more user-friendly experience for those who may not be tech-savvy.

Conclusion

In summary, I believe that there is a market for the “Better Planet App”. Challenges that came up were gaining a deep understanding users' perspectives, mapping out a realistic and comprehensive user journey. Based on feedback from user testing and the Maze report, identified issues such as misclicks, confusing navigation, and visual design problems.
